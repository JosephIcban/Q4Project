Timothee Chalamet


Introduction

Timothée Hal Chalamet, born in Manhattan to Nicole Flender, a real estate broker and dancer, and Marc Chalamet, a UNICEF editor. His mother, from New York, is Jewish, of Russian Jewish and Austrian Jewish descent, while his father, from Nîmes, France, has French and English roots. He has a sister, Pauline Chalamet, an actress, and is related to director Rodman Flender and screenwriter Harold Flender.


He debuted in 2014, appearing in Jason Reitman's "Men, Women & Children" and Matthew McConaughey's "Interstellar". He then starred in indie films like "The Adderall Diaries" and "One and Two", and played notable roles on stage and television, including in "Miss Stevens", "The Talls", "Prodigal Son", "Law & Order", "Royal Pains", and "Homeland".


In 2017, he rose to prominence with notable supporting roles in "Hostiles" and "Lady Bird". He also starred as Elio in "Call Me by Your Name", earning widespread acclaim and nominations for the Academy Award and Golden Globe for Best Actor.
